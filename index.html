Anonymous Case Study: Optimal Marketing Spend and Strategy

This case study focuses on optimizing the marketing spend and strategy for an e-commerce retailer, ensuring maximum return on investment across channels.

Overview

We worked with a leading e-commerce retailer to analyze their advertising spend across various channels and determine the optimal cost-per-order percentage (COP%) for maximizing profit.

Our Approach

  • Data Cleaning: Processed the data, removing unreliable values and grouping by channel and date.
  • Segmentation: Grouped data by dates and performed regression analysis to observe trends in relation to ad spend.
  • Analysis: Observed differences across quarters to eliminate noise from high-demand periods, providing a clearer picture of cost-efficiency.

Key Results

Our analysis showed the following optimal cost-per-order percentage for maximizing profit in various quarters:

  • Q1: 0.45-0.65 COP%
  • Q2: 0.55-0.75 COP%
  • Q3: 0.60-0.80 COP%
  • Q4: 0.50-0.70 COP%

We recommended a daily marketing spend of 4000 to 8000 Euros during low-demand quarters and focused on SEM and Paid Media optimization for long-term brand awareness.

Conclusion

By focusing on the optimal COP% and carefully adjusting our SEM strategies across quarters, we helped the retailer achieve substantial profitability, particularly in key periods like Q4. Further analysis on marketing mix modeling is recommended for future strategy refinement.
